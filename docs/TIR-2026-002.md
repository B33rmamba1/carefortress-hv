## Executive Summary

Over 10 days of honeypot operations (July 13-23, 2026), CareFortress canary tokens have mapped a complete credential theft supply chain from initial harvest through marketplace distribution to end-user exploitation. The ecosystem operates in three tiers: bulk harvesters that scrape exposed credential files at scale, operators who run their own scanning infrastructure, and marketplace buyers who purchase stolen credentials for specific objectives without ever contacting the source.

Nine of twenty AWS canary token pool slots have been triggered by nine distinct IPs across three countries, three distinct toolkits, and three distinct exploitation objectives. Two direct credential handoffs and four marketplace transfers have been confirmed through IP-to-token correlation and behavioral analysis.

## Buyer Segmentation

The marketplace serves at least three distinct buyer segments:

### Segment 1: Spam Infrastructure (SES/SNS)

**Objective:** Acquire AWS accounts with email sending (SES) and SMS (SNS) capabilities for spam, phishing, and smishing campaigns.

**API validation sequence:** GetCallerIdentity → GetSendQuota → GetSMSAttributes

**IPs:** 146.70.187.210, 103.81.231.195, 23.234.112.204, 23.234.115.81

**Toolkit:** Shared Boto3/1.42.70 package on Windows Server 2022.

### Segment 2: AI Compute Theft (Bedrock)

**Objective:** Acquire AWS accounts with Bedrock access for free AI model inference (Claude, Llama, Titan, Stable Diffusion).

**API calls observed:** InvokeModel (direct model use), ListFoundationModels (model enumeration)

**IPs:** 206.125.53.55 (aiohttp, residential), 162.158.x.x (Cloudflare WARP, no UA)

**Note:** This represents an emerging trend in cloud credential abuse. Stolen keys with Bedrock permissions provide free access to expensive AI models. The shift from crypto mining to AI compute theft reflects market economics — AI inference is now more valuable per compute-hour than cryptocurrency mining.

### Segment 3: Account Reconnaissance (Organizations)

**Objective:** Enumerate AWS account structure for potential account takeover or privilege escalation.

**API calls observed:** GetCallerIdentity, GetAccount (AWS Organizations)

**IPs:** 54.198.166.197 (AWS EC2, us-east-1)

**Toolkit:** Boto3/1.42.97, Python 3.9.10, Windows 10 — distinct from the spam operators' toolkit. Running directly on EC2 indicates comfort operating within AWS infrastructure.

## Detection Architecture

### What Worked

| Layer | Detection | Latency |
|---|---|---|
| Spider trap (Cloudflare Worker) | Initial scan, honeypot content served | < 1 second |
| Canary hostname callbacks | Post-exploitation infrastructure probing | < 1 second |
| AWS canary tokens (Thinkst CloudTrail) | Credential testing | 5-15 minutes |
| Per-visitor deterministic tokens | Marketplace transfer confirmation | Correlation at analysis time |

### Detection Gap Identified

AWS canary token alerts from Thinkst are delivered via email only. They do not currently flow into the Wazuh SIEM. Attackers who test credentials without ever touching carefortress.dev (all Tier 3 buyers) generate no Wazuh alerts. The InvokeModel call from 206.125.53.55 and the ListFoundationModels calls from the WARP attacker produced zero SIEM visibility.

**Remediation:** Add Thinkst webhook integration to route CloudTrail alerts through the existing Cloudflare tunnel to the Wazuh listener, or build an email-to-syslog pipeline for canary alert emails.

---

## Recommendations

1. **For AWS defenders:** Monitor CloudTrail for GetSMSAttributes, GetSendQuota, ListFoundationModels, and InvokeModel from unexpected IPs. These are high-signal credential validation calls.

2. **For honeypot operators:** Per-visitor deterministic tokens are essential for marketplace analysis. Without unique-per-IP canary tokens, the handoff and marketplace transfer confirmations in this report would be impossible.

3. **For incident responders:** Treat any .env or .aws/credentials exposure as compromised within 60 seconds. The fastest operator in this dataset tested stolen keys within one minute of harvest.

4. **For threat intelligence teams:** The credential marketplace now serves distinct buyer segments with different objectives (spam, AI compute, account recon). A single leaked credential may generate activity from multiple unrelated threat actors.
